About

I played a key role in developing the Scoring System for the 13th PaDaeng National Angklung Music Competition, focusing on redesigning the assessment system to be accessible online. My responsibilities included ensuring accurate evaluations, managing the elimination of extreme scores, and providing recommendations for a transparent scoring system. My analysis served as a crucial foundation for the event chief organizers and the advisory board in making well-informed decisions.

I successfully completed this format and it was used in the competition.

Context

Lomba Musik Angklung PaDaeng (LMAP) is a biennial event organized by Student Activity Units Keluarga Besar Bumi Siliwangi. Held at Universitas Pendidikan Indonesia (UPI), this competition serves as a platform for participants from various levels, including kindergarten, elementary school, junior high school, senior high school, university students, and the general public at the national level.

Problem

Currently, the committee uses a manual scoring system that collects scores from multiple judges separately. This process often leads to potential calculation errors, ties in participants’ scores, and difficulty in adjusting extremely high or low scores. Moreover, the offline scoring format complicates updates and quick distribution of results.

Business Problem

To enhance accuracy and efficiency in judging, the committee needs a scoring system that can:

  • To compare three assessment methods:
    1. Summing all scores from each judge
    2. Eliminating the highest and lowest scores
    3. Calculating ranking scores from each judge
  • To introduce standardization features to prevent identical scores among participants.
  • To transition the assessment format from offline to an accessible online system.
  • To refine the previous assessment formula, making it more readable and user-friendly.
  • To develop an automated system where users only need to input data, and the legger, report card, and champion’s certificate are generated seamlessly.

Result

The implementation of the new Scoring System for the 13th PaDaeng National Angklung Music Competition successfully addressed the key challenges posed by the previous manual assessment method. The system provided significant improvements in accuracy, efficiency, fairness, and transparency, resulting in the following outcomes:

  • Eliminated Calculation Errors & Score Discrepancies: The automated scoring process minimized human errors and inconsistencies, ensuring accurate results and reducing disputes over participant rankings.
  • Balanced & Fair Evaluation: By implementing the elimination of extreme scores and ranking-based assessments, the system ensured a fairer evaluation process, reducing bias from outlier scores given by individual judges.
  • Prevented Judging Manipulation & Bribery: The introduction of score standardization and automated calculations prevented judges from unfairly increasing or decreasing participant scores due to personal bias or external influences such as bribery. This ensured that all participants were evaluated solely based on their performance, without interference from subjective scoring tendencies.
  • Standardized & Readable Assessment: The redesigned formula improved readability, making it easier for both judges and event organizers to interpret scores effectively. This formula ensures that scores are normalized within a defined range, preventing extreme variations in grading and maintaining a fair evaluation process.
  • Enhanced Accessibility & Efficiency: Transitioning from an offline to an online system allowed judges to input scores digitally, streamlining data collection and reducing processing time. Results were instantly available, eliminating delays in score tabulation.
  • Automated Report Generation: The system generated legger, report cards, and champion certificates automatically, reducing manual workload and expediting the award announcement process.

By integrating this automated and standardized scoring system, the competition achieved a faster, more reliable, and corruption-free assessment process, ensuring that every participant was judged fairly based on their actual performance.
